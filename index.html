What is BMI ?


Body mass index (BMI) is a measure of a person's weight in relation to their height. BMI is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. For example, a BMI of 25 means 25kg/m2.


BMI is often considered an indicator of body fatness. However, it's a surrogate measure of body fat because it measures excess weight rather than excess fat.
